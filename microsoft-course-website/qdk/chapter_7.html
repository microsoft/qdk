6.1 Second Quantization and Fermionic Operators

In second quantization, a quantum chemistry Hamiltonian is written as:

𝐻 =∑𝑝⁢𝑞ℎ𝑝⁢𝑞 ⁡𝑎†𝑝⁢𝑎𝑞 +12⁢∑𝑝⁢𝑞⁢𝑟⁢𝑠ℎ𝑝⁢𝑞⁢𝑟⁢𝑠 ⁡𝑎†𝑝⁢𝑎†𝑞⁢𝑎𝑟⁢𝑎𝑠

where 𝑎†𝑝 (create) and 𝑎𝑝 (annihilate) are fermionic operators satisfying the anti-commutation relations:

{𝑎𝑝,𝑎†𝑞} =𝛿𝑝⁢𝑞, {𝑎𝑝,𝑎𝑞} =0

The one-electron integrals ℎ𝑝⁢𝑞 encode kinetic energy + electron-nuclear attraction; the two-electron integrals ℎ𝑝⁢𝑞⁢𝑟⁢𝑠 encode electron-electron repulsion.

6.2 Jordan-Wigner Transform

The Jordan-Wigner (JW) transform maps fermionic operators to qubit operators:

𝑎†𝑝 =(∏𝑗<𝑝𝑍𝑗)⁢𝑋𝑝−𝑖⁢𝑌𝑝2, 𝑎𝑝 =(∏𝑗<𝑝𝑍𝑗)⁢𝑋𝑝+𝑖⁢𝑌𝑝2

This ensures that anti-commutation relations are preserved via the Z-string (Jordan-Wigner string). One qubit per spin-orbital.
