Why localize?

Canonical molecular orbitals from SCF are delocalized across the entire molecule â€” they're eigenstates of the Fock operator, optimized for energy, not for physical interpretability. For active space selection, delocalized orbitals obscure which electrons are strongly correlated. Localization rotates the orbital basis to produce spatially compact, chemically meaningful orbitals without changing the total energy or wavefunction.

Part 1: Reducing to the valence space

Before localizing, we reduce to the valence space â€” the chemically relevant frontier orbitals. The Nâ‚‚ cc-pvdz calculation produces 28 orbitals, most of which are high-energy virtuals that contribute negligibly to chemistry. Working in the valence space is a prerequisite for localization in this workflow.

compute_valence_space_parameters() is a utility that computes the number of valence electrons and orbitals for a given wavefunction. The qdk_valence active space selector then applies these to extract the valence subspace. Here, let's compute the valence space parameters for stretched Nâ‚‚ and apply the valence selector.
